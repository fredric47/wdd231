What is Artemis?

NASA’s Artemis program is the United States’ effort to return humans to the Moon and build a long‑term presence there. Unlike the Apollo missions, which focused on short visits, Artemis is designed for sustained exploration — using the Moon as a training ground for future missions to Mars.

Artemis will land the first woman and the first person of color on the lunar surface, explore the Moon’s south pole, and develop technologies that support deep‑space travel. The program combines NASA’s spacecraft and rockets with major contributions from commercial partners and international space agencies.


So how are they going to do it?

Artemis I

An uncrewed test flight launched in 2022. Orion traveled around the Moon and back, proving the Space Launch System (SLS) rocket and spacecraft systems work for future crewed missions.

Artemis II

The first crewed mission of the program. A team of astronauts will fly around the Moon, testing life‑support, navigation, and deep‑space operations to prepare for lunar landings.

Artemis III

Planned as the mission that will return humans to the lunar surface, targeting the Moon’s south pole. It will use a Human Landing System to place astronauts on the surface and begin long‑term exploration.
