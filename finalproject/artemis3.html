Artemis III

🌑 Artemis III — Detailed Overview
🌕 Mission Purpose
Artemis III aims to land astronauts on the Moon for the first time since Apollo 17 in 1972. It focuses on exploring the Moon’s south pole, a region rich in water ice and scientific potential.

🚀 What the Mission Will Do
Launch astronauts aboard Orion using the SLS rocket

Dock with the Human Landing System (HLS) — a modified SpaceX Starship — in lunar orbit

Land two astronauts near the south pole

Conduct multiple moonwalks to collect samples and test tools

Deploy scientific instruments and technology demonstrations

Study water ice in permanently shadowed craters

🧪 Key Mission Goals
Explore the south pole for resources

Test new spacesuits designed for mobility and durability

Demonstrate long‑duration surface operations

Begin building the foundation for a sustained lunar presence

🛰️ Long‑Term Impact
Artemis III marks the beginning of NASA’s plan to establish a lunar base camp, support international partnerships, and prepare for human missions to Mars.
